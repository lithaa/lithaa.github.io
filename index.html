Litha Ashforth, soprano, is known for her “spellbinding” presence on stage. Her work spans operatic vocals, sacred dance, haptics, custom software, and field recordings. A recipient of numerous competitive honors, she has been distinguished in competition by the Performing Arts Scholarship Foundation at the Music Academy of the West, the Maestro Award from World Strides Heritage Festival, and is a recipient of the Dean’s Scholarship at the New England Conservatory of Music.

Litha has been performing since her preschool graduation. Most recently, she performed with the Boston Symphony Orchestra, and was a featured soloist at the Tournament of Roses, Music on Norway Pond Festival, Judson Memorial Church in NYC, and the Vietnam War Memorial. Her credits in opera, musical theatre, and film are numerous. This spring, she starred as Adele in Die Fledermaus at the Plimpton Shattuck Black Box Theatre, and depicted the lead role in the world premiere of Order: A Play by Anna Abondolo and Jon Starks.

Litha is also passionate about leadership, education, and activism. She currently serves as Student Body President and founder of the Student Embassy at the New England Conservatory. As an activist, she works with organizations including Harvard Street Team and Judson Bail Out to provide necessary resources for people experiencing homelessness, and aids such organizations in creating public concerts. Litha currently teaches music programming under the direction of Walter Bender, the former director of the MIT Media Lab. As a part of this work, she has developed implementations for bioacoustics pedagogy within Music Blocks, Bender’s in-development programming language for music education.

Her work as a composer addresses technology, ancestral memory, and bioacoustics. Her most recent piece, “Shadow Kick” premiered May 2022 in Jordan Hall.
